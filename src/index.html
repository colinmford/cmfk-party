Travel

By Air: NYC airports are John F. Kennedy International (JFK), Newark (EWR), and LaGuardia (LGA). All airports are serviced by taxis (follow the signs to the taxi stand), car services and public transportation that will whisk you into the island of Manhattan in 30-60 minutes.

By Train: Travel into NYC by Amtrak to New York Pennsylvania Station (NYP), then take a taxi or the subway (A/C/E or 1/2/3) to your hotel.

By Car: From points south, take the New Jersey Turnpike. Travel into Manhattan via the Holland Tunnel (lower Manhattan), Lincoln Tunnel (midtown), or the George Washington Bridge (uptown). All have tolls. Parking is limited in the city, but do-able. Street parking availability and rules will vary, so check street signs. Parking garages are also available all over the city.

Getting to the Venue

The Aqua Azul will be moored at Chelsea Piers, Pier 60, nearest to 20th street and 11th avenue. She will be docked on the north side of the pier (right side, if you are facing the Hudson River), halfway back.

By Cab or Car Service (recommended): Just tell your cab driver to take you to Chelsea Piers, pier 60. It is the second parking entrance on the right. Cabs are allowed to enter the parking garage without cost. It's that easy!

By Car: The Chelsea Piers have covered parking. Park at Pier 60, the second parking entrance on the right. It costs $56 to park for the duration of the wedding. If you plan to drive, please arrive early, as parking may be limited on the day of the wedding. More on Parking.

By Public Transportation: The closest subway stop is the 14th Street A/C/E/L subway station. Exit at 16th Street & 8th Avenue. On exiting the subway station, walk west to 11th avenue, and walk uptown to the Chelsea Piers entrance at 18th street. From within the Chelsea Piers complex, continue uptown and follow the signs for Pier 60. The walk takes approximately 10 minutes.

Other Things to Do

We appreciate greatly that you are traveling to celebrate with us at our wedding! We would like to suggest a few things that we enjoy doing in our city.

Chelsea Market has a variety of shops and restaurants housed within a rehabbed ice and biscuit factory.

The Observatory at the new World Trade Center offers an AMAZING view of the entire island of Manhattan and it's surrounding boroughs.

The Metropolitan Museum of Art is a world renowned museum with collections ranging from antiquities to modern art. An exciting looking Rodin exhibition opens the day of our wedding!
